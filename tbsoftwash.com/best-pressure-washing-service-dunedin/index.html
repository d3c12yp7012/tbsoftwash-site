FAQ's

Frequently asked questions about professional pressure washing.

Can I Damage My Flatwork When Pressure Washing?
Yes, when using a power washer to clean concrete, bricks, wood, or paving stones, it is possible to cause significant, visible damage, which is usually in the form of pitting, lines or general surface degradation. You can also damage the mortar between bricks and disperse the joint sand between paving stones. A professional pressure cleaning company should have a clear understanding of acceptable psi on different surfaces.
How Often Should I pressure Wash My Driveway and Sidewalks?

In order to maintain your flatwork and keep it looking clean and fresh, you should aim to pressure clean your driveway at least once per year. It is also a good idea to pressure wash your driveway any time that it starts to look particularly dirty or overgrown with moss or weeds. Not only can dirt, moss, and weeds be unsightly, but if left untreated, they can become a potential slip hazards.

I Dont Have an Outside Tap or Water Service Can You Still Pressure Wash?

While it is common practice to utilize the customers water, we are capable of transporting all of the water required for the project.

What Is The Difference Between Pressure Washing and Power Washing?

The main difference with power washing is the heat. The power washing machine uses a water heater, whereas the water in a pressure washer is cold.

Oil & Grease Stain Removal

Pores on concrete and pavers easily seep in oil and grease causing unsightly stains that are difficult to remove. Letting the oil or grease sit for a long time only makes removing these stains more difficult. Our wash technicians use the best hot water washing equipment and commercially available detergents but we still can not guarantee to completely remove some oil and grease stains. However, we can certainly lighten them and make these stains less noticeable. We let our degreasers dwell on the stain, gently scrub the stain, and, then we rinse using steaming hot water at low pressure.

Gum Removal

While hot water pressure washers are the go to tool for gum removal, it’s not the water pressure but rather the heat that melts the gum away. Using a cold water pressure washer at high pressure can damage the concrete. It is also imperative to remove the gum as soon as you see it because gum that is left for an extended period can stain the surface due to the acidity in the saliva of the gum as well as the chemical makeup of the chewing gum, if left on the porous concrete, it can etch the concrete surface causing it to be more susceptible to the environment.
